SAHIH BUKHARI

Volume 7, Book 72, Number 789:

Narrated Abdullah bin Umar:
Allah's Apostle said, "Today I saw myself in a dream near the Ka'ba. I saw a whitish brown man,
the handsomest of all brown men you might ever see. He had the most beautiful Limma (hair
hanging down to the earlobes) you might ever see. He had combed it and it was dripping water; and
he was performing the Tawaf around the Kaba leaning on two men or on the shoulders of two men. l
asked, "Who is this?" It was said. "Messiah, the son of Mary." Suddenly I saw a curly-haired man,
blind in the right eye which looked like a protruding out grape. I asked, "Who is this?" It was said,
"He is Masiah Ad-Dajjal."
Volume 7, Book 72, Number 790a:
Narrated Anas :
The hair of the Prophet used to hang down up to his shoulders.
Volume 7, Book 72, Number 790b:
Narrated Anas:
The head-hair of the Prophet used to hang down to his shoulders.
