SAHIH BUKHARI

Volume 7, Book 67, Number 398:

Narrated Abu Qatada:
that once he was with Allah's Apostle (on the way to Mecca). When he had covered some of the
way to Mecca, he and some companions of his, who were in the state of lhram. remained behind the
Prophet while Abu Qatada himself was not in the state of Ihram. Abu Qatada, seeing an onager rode
his horse and asked his companions to hand him a whip, but they refused. He then asked them to
hand him his spear, but they refused. Then he took it himself and attacked the onager and killed it.
Some of the Companions of Allah's Apostle ate of it, but some others refused to eat. When they met
Allah's Apostle they asked him about that. He said, "It was meal given to you by Allah."
